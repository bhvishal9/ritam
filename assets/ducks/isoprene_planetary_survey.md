# Isoprene: A Comprehensive Planetary Survey

Issued under the authority of the Department of the Inner Tide, Bureau of Planetary Affairs, in the 4,410th Standard Cycle. This survey supersedes the previous comprehensive survey of the 4,288th Cycle and incorporates the findings of the Long Census, the Glassine Geological Survey, and the Reedwater Hydrological Survey.

This document is the standard reference for Isoprene as the ancestral and capital world of the Quack-Federa-tion. It is intended for use by administrators, scholars, diplomats, and senior naval personnel. A condensed edition is available for general circulation.

## Part I: Physical Description

### 1.1 Astrographic Position

Isoprene is the first planet of the Poly-Prime system, in the Rubber Rim sector. The Poly-Prime star is a stable G-type main sequence star with an estimated remaining lifespan of approximately 4.1 billion standard cycles. Isoprene orbits at a mean distance of 1.04 standard astronomical units, with an orbital period of 367.2 standard days and a rotational period of 23.7 standard hours.

The Isoprene system contains, in addition to Isoprene itself, the inner planet The Mold (the historical molding world of the Duck species), three minor planets (Squeak Major, Squeak Mid, Squeak Minor — the latter being a moon of Elastos in the neighboring Vulcan-Alpha system but historically associated with Isoprene), and an outer belt of icy debris known as the Frosted Ring.

### 1.2 Physical Characteristics

Isoprene has an equatorial diameter of 13,128 standard kilometers, a mean density of 5.7 g/cm³, and a surface gravity of 1.02 standard gravities. The axial tilt is 19.4 degrees, producing moderate seasonal variation. The magnetic field is strong and stable, with a dipole moment approximately 1.4 times that of the standard reference world.

The atmosphere is a nitrogen-oxygen mix with approximately 21% oxygen, 77% nitrogen, and a complex mix of trace gases including elevated levels of isoprene vapor (from which the planet takes its name). Atmospheric pressure at sea level is 1.06 standard atmospheres. The mean surface temperature is 14.3°C, with regional variation from -42°C in the polar regions to +38°C in the equatorial Rubber Belt.

### 1.3 Hydrosphere

Approximately 64% of Isoprene's surface is covered by liquid water. The major bodies are:

- **The Great Squeak Ocean** — the largest body, covering most of the western hemisphere. The Great Squeak Ocean is named for a low-frequency standing acoustic phenomenon generated by interaction between the prevailing currents and the resonant bedrock of the Squeak Plateau.
- **The Reedwater Sea** — a partially enclosed sea on the eastern continent, fringed with extensive reed marshes. The capital city of Splashport is on the Reedwater Sea's southern shore.
- **The Glassine Cliffs Strait** — a narrow strait separating the eastern continent from the Glassine Peninsula, of considerable historical and ceremonial significance.

Major rivers include the Stillwater (longest at 4,200 km), the Quickwater, the Murkwater, the Slowsong, and the Sapflow. The Sapflow is unique in carrying suspended polyisoprene from the polymer forests, giving it a milky-white color and high economic significance.

### 1.4 Geology

Isoprene's geology is dominated by an unusual high-flexibility crust, attributed to the planet's exceptionally high concentration of long-chain polymers in the upper mantle. Earthquakes on Isoprene are typically slow and rolling rather than sharp; major structural collapses are rare. The Glassine Cliffs are an exception — a region of crystalline silicate where the surface has, through geological processes not fully understood, vitrified to a glass-like consistency over an area of approximately 80,000 square kilometers.

There are seven major tectonic plates. Plate motion is slow (approximately 1.4 cm per cycle) and orderly. The major mountain ranges are the Reedback Range (eastern continent, max elevation 7,100 m), the Mold Mountains (named for their proximity to the line of sight toward the planet The Mold, max elevation 5,800 m), and the Sapwood Highlands (a series of forested high plateaus, max elevation 3,200 m).

## Part II: Biosphere

### 2.1 Flora

Isoprene's flora is dominated by polymer-producing plants of the family *Polyisoprenaceae*. The polymer forests cover approximately 22% of the land surface and are the source of nearly all polyisoprene used in Federation industry. The largest forest, the Western Sapwood, covers more than four million square kilometers.

Notable plant species include:

- **The Reed-of-Council** (*Phragmites consilium*) — a tall, hollow-stemmed marsh plant whose dried stems are used in the ceremonial reed-ballots of the Tide Council. The Reed-of-Council is found only in the marshes of the Reedwater Sea and is protected under the Reedwater Conservation Act.
- **The Sap-Drinker Tree** (*Lacticosus magnus*) — the dominant species of the polymer forests. Mature specimens can exceed 80 meters in height and produce up to 400 liters of polyisoprene sap per cycle.
- **The Glassine Lichen** (*Vitreum tenax*) — a slow-growing lichen that colonizes the surfaces of the Glassine Cliffs, contributing to their characteristic blue-green coloration.
- **The Squeak Grass** (*Acutivox prata*) — a grass species whose blades produce audible high-frequency tones when disturbed by wind. The Squeak Grass meadows of the central continent are a popular tourist destination.

### 2.2 Fauna

Isoprene's pre-sapient fauna is diverse and well documented. The dominant terrestrial vertebrate group is the *Anseriformes*, of which the sapient Ducks are a single (though dominant) species. Other notable *Anseriformes* include:

- **The Greater Pondwhale** (*Cetanseris maxima*) — a freshwater giant inhabiting the largest rivers and lakes. Despite the name, the Greater Pondwhale is not closely related to the Deep-Singers of Deluge.
- **The Riverdrake** (*Anseridracus fluvialis*) — a long-necked, predatory bird-analogue of the eastern continent's river systems. The Riverdrake is the largest carnivorous vertebrate on Isoprene.
- **The Skitterbill** (*Parvanseris saltans*) — a small, fast-moving ground-dweller that travels in flocks of up to ten thousand individuals.

Marine fauna is dominated by the *Mollusca* and the *Echinodermata*. The Great Squeak Ocean is home to the Squeak Reef, the largest coral analogue in the system, covering an area of approximately 280,000 square kilometers.

### 2.3 The Ducks

The sapient Ducks (*Anseriformes sapiens*) evolved on Isoprene approximately 2.4 million cycles ago, in the river systems of the central continent. The earliest archaeological evidence of Duck tool use dates from approximately 80,000 cycles ago. The earliest evidence of agricultural settlement dates from approximately 12,000 cycles ago, in the floodplain of the Stillwater River.

The thirteen Paddles trace their origin to thirteen distinct cultural-geographic groupings that emerged during the Settlement Period (approximately 12,000 to 6,000 cycles ago). The Paddles in approximate order of foundation are: Stillwater, Reedsmith, Sapflow, Glassine, Mold, Hardshell, Saltreed, Squeak, Bowsprit, Pinfeather, Marsh-of-Reeds, Tidewalker, and Bubble-Rider.

For details on Paddle customs, governance, and contemporary distribution, see *Compendium of the Paddles*, QFH-0287.

## Part III: Human Geography

### 3.1 Population

The total population of Isoprene at the most recent Long Census (4,408th Cycle) was 1.84 billion, of which approximately 1.79 billion were Ducks, 38 million were Echo-Folk on long-term cultural exchange, and the remainder were a mix of other Federation species and resident non-citizens. Population density is highly uneven: the Reedwater basin and the Sapflow valley contain approximately 60% of the total population on less than 8% of the land area.

Population growth in the most recent cycle was +0.4%, the lowest rate since the immediate post-war Reckoning period.

### 3.2 Major Settlements

**Splashport** (population 84 million) is the capital of the Quack-Federa-tion and the largest city on Isoprene. Splashport sits on the southern shore of the Reedwater Sea, at the mouth of the Slowsong River. The city is built on a network of floating platforms and bridged islets, with a substantial submerged section. The floating Council Platform, seat of the Tide Council, is at the city's geographic center.

**Buoyant** (population 41 million) is the second city of Isoprene and the principal shipbuilding center. The Buoyant Yards are located along a 60-kilometer stretch of the Buoyant Estuary on the western coast of the eastern continent.

**Glassine** (population 12 million) is the city built into and beneath the Glassine Cliffs. It is the seat of the Still Water (the supreme court) and the principal university city of the Federation. The University of Glassine, founded in the 3,201st Cycle, is the oldest continuously operating institution of higher learning in known space.

**Sapwood** (population 9 million) is the principal city of the polymer-forest region, and the center of the Federation's polyisoprene processing industry.

**Squeak Plateau** (population 7 million) is a high-altitude city on the western continent, notable for its association with the discovery of the Squeak Drive principle (though the device itself was developed on Squeak Minor, in the Vulcan-Alpha system).

Smaller settlements of cultural or strategic significance include Stillwater-Mouth (the birthplace of Commodore Waddles), Reedmarsh (the seat of the Reedsmith Paddle), and Hardshell Landing (the seat of the Hardshell Paddle).

### 3.3 Transportation

Inter-city transportation on Isoprene is dominated by surface watercraft on the extensive river-and-canal network and by aerial vehicles for longer routes. The Trans-Continental Canal, completed in the 4,021st Cycle, connects the Reedwater Sea to the Great Squeak Ocean across the central continent, a distance of 3,400 kilometers.

Off-world transportation is centered on three orbital lift facilities: Splashport Lift, Buoyant Lift, and the smaller Sapwood Lift. The Splashport Lift is the largest civilian orbital lift in the Federation, with a sustained throughput of approximately 12,000 metric tons per standard day.

## Part IV: Economy

### 4.1 Sectoral Composition

The Isoprene economy is the largest in the Federation, accounting for approximately 41% of total Federation economic output. By sector:

- Polymer extraction and processing: 18%
- Shipbuilding (primarily at Buoyant): 14%
- Government and administration: 11%
- Aquaculture and fisheries: 9%
- Higher education and research: 6%
- Manufacturing (non-shipbuilding): 22%
- Services and other: 20%

### 4.2 The Polymer Industry

Polyisoprene from Isoprene's polymer forests is the structural foundation of Federation industry. The principal harvesting cooperatives are the Western Sapwood Cooperative (the largest), the Eastern Sapwood Cooperative, and the Sapflow Valley Cooperative. The cooperatives are organized under the Polymer Industry Compact of the 4,103rd Cycle, which establishes harvesting quotas to ensure long-term forest sustainability.

Refined polyisoprene is the principal export of Isoprene to the rest of the Federation. The principal customers are the Buoyant Yards (for ship construction), the Vulcanized Works on Elastos (for further compounding with Elastos's vulcanized polymers), and the construction industry of Latheria.

### 4.3 Shipbuilding

The Buoyant Yards are the largest shipbuilding facility in the Federation. The Yards employ approximately 380,000 workers and have a sustained capital-vessel construction capacity of approximately fourteen vessels per cycle. The Yards are publicly owned and operated by the Admiralty under the Buoyant Yards Charter of the 4,015th Cycle.

The principal current production programs are the Mark-XII Battlebill (heavy capital vessel, replacing the aging Mark-XI), the Mark-IV Survey Cruiser, and the Mark-VII Escort. The Wave Generator Refit Program, currently retrofitting the entire surviving Wave Generator fleet with the Mark-VI generator, is the largest single procurement program in current Federation military history.

### 4.4 Higher Education and Research

Isoprene hosts twenty-three accredited universities. The University of Glassine, the University of Splashport, and the Naval Academy at Buoyant are the largest. The Federation Research Institutes — a network of seventeen specialized research bodies funded directly by the Department of the Inner Tide — are also concentrated on Isoprene, with the principal facilities at Glassine, Stillwater-Mouth, and Sapwood.

## Part V: Government and Administration

### 5.1 Status

Isoprene is the capital world of the Quack-Federa-tion. It is not itself a Paddle or a colony; it is the territory in which the thirteen Paddles and the Federation institutions are physically based. Administratively, Isoprene is divided into one hundred and forty-seven Districts, each of which sends one Districtor to the Isoprene Assembly.

The Isoprene Assembly is responsible for matters not reserved to the Federation Tide Council or to the Paddles — primarily, planetary infrastructure, environmental regulation, and inter-District coordination.

### 5.2 The Federation Capital Zone

The Federation Capital Zone is a special administrative district encompassing the central islands of Splashport, the Council Platform, the executive offices of the Hydromarch, and the chambers of the Still Water (which, despite its location at Glassine, is administratively part of the Capital Zone). The Capital Zone is administered directly by the Department of the Inner Tide and is not subject to the Isoprene Assembly.

### 5.3 Law Enforcement and Defense

Domestic law enforcement on Isoprene is the responsibility of the Isoprene Constabulary, a civilian body reporting to the Department of Stillness. Planetary defense is the responsibility of the First Squadron of the Federation Fleet, which maintains its principal base in geostationary orbit above Splashport. The First Squadron is by tradition the most prestigious posting in the Fleet, and is typically commanded by the senior serving Commodore.

## Part VI: History

### 6.1 Pre-Federation Period

For approximately 12,000 cycles before the founding of the Quack-Federa-tion, the Ducks of Isoprene lived in a succession of regional polities — kingdoms, republics, and confederations of varying scale. The most important pre-Federation polities were the Reedwater League (a maritime confederation of the eastern continent, ascendant from approximately 6,000 cycles ago to approximately 3,500 cycles ago), the Sapwood Kingdom (the unifying state of the central continent from approximately 4,200 cycles ago to the founding of the Federation), and the Glassine Republic (the longest continuously existing polity, extant from approximately 5,800 cycles ago to the founding of the Federation, and arguably continuous with the modern District of Glassine).

The Reedwater League is the polity from which most modern Federation institutions trace direct descent. The League's Council of Reedmasters is the institutional ancestor of the Tide Council; the League's office of Tidewalker is the institutional ancestor of the Hydromarch; the League's High Court of the Quiet Waters is the institutional ancestor of the Still Water.

### 6.2 Founding of the Federation

The Quack-Federa-tion was founded in the 3,021st Standard Cycle, by the Compact of the Reeds, signed at Reedmarsh by the representatives of the thirteen Paddles. The Compact established the basic governmental framework that, with substantial amendment, remains in force today.

The immediate impetus for the Compact was the Pondwater Crisis of the 3,019th Cycle, in which a series of regional polities had come into conflict over access to a critical aquifer in the central continent. The crisis demonstrated the inadequacy of the existing inter-polity arbitration mechanisms and produced sufficient political will for federation.

### 6.3 The Period of Exploration

The first thousand cycles of the Federation are known as the Period of Exploration. During this period, the Federation developed the Hydro-thruster and Squeak Drive technologies that enabled interstellar travel and dispatched the great survey expeditions that mapped the Eight Sectors and established the first colonies.

The principal milestones of the Period of Exploration are:

- The first successful Hydro-thruster test, 3,109th Cycle, at the Buoyant Yards.
- The first successful interstellar flight, 3,247th Cycle, by the prototype *First Paddle*.
- The founding of the Latheria Colony, 3,401st Cycle.
- The founding of the Whirlpoolia Outpost, 3,489th Cycle.
- The discovery of the Loofah Line, 3,612th Cycle.
- The first contact with the Echo-Folk, 3,789th Cycle.
- The Treaty of the Long Hum, 3,847th Cycle.
- The founding of the Wave-Rider city-states, 3,901st Cycle.

### 6.4 The Modern Period

The modern period of Federation history is conventionally dated from the conclusion of the Great Duck Wars in the 4,074th Cycle. For the history of the Wars themselves, see *The Great Duck Wars: An Extended History*, QFH-0014-EXT.

The principal developments of the modern period have been the post-war constitutional reforms (4,082nd Cycle), the integration of the Wave-Rider city-states as full Federation members (4,156th Cycle), the Reedwater Conservation Act (4,201st Cycle), the Pendweather Accord regarding Bristle (4,289th Cycle), the founding of the Perfuma distilleries (4,312th Cycle), and the ongoing Wave Generator Refit Program (begun 4,395th Cycle).

## Part VII: Culture

### 7.1 Language

The standard language of Isoprene and of the Federation is Modern Squeakish, descended from the Reedwater dialect of pre-Federation Squeakish. Modern Squeakish is the language of government, commerce, and inter-Paddle communication. The thirteen Paddles also maintain Paddle-specific dialects of varying degrees of mutual intelligibility; the most divergent is Hardshell Squeakish, which has been classified by some linguists as a separate language.

Modern Squeakish is a tonal language with a phonemic inventory dominated by labial and glottal sounds appropriate to the Duck bill. Written Squeakish uses a syllabic script of 247 characters.

### 7.2 Religion

The dominant religious tradition of Isoprene is the Way of the Tides, a non-theistic philosophical-ritual tradition that emphasizes harmony with natural cycles, ancestor veneration, and meditative practice. Approximately 67% of the population of Isoprene identifies as a practitioner of the Way of the Tides; an additional 18% identifies as a practitioner of one of several smaller traditions; the remainder identifies as non-religious.

The principal ceremonial occasions of the Way of the Tides are the four Tide Days, marking the equinoxes and solstices, and the Day of Stillness, observed on the anniversary of the signing of the Loofah Accord.

### 7.3 Arts

Isoprene has rich traditions in poetry, music, architecture, and the visual arts. The classical Squeakish poetic forms are the Tideline (a fourteen-syllable couplet with strict tonal requirements) and the Long Ripple (a longer narrative form, traditionally recited rather than read). The standard reference anthology is the *Garland of Tidelines*, first compiled in the 3,401st Cycle and now in its forty-seventh edition.

Squeakish classical music is built around the cycle of the seven Tones, derived from the seven primary resonant frequencies of the Reed-of-Council. The principal orchestral form is the Tideflow, typically organized in twelve movements corresponding to the twelve months of the standard cycle.

Squeakish architecture emphasizes flexible polyisoprene-based construction, integrated water features, and harmonious proportions derived from the Golden Ripple — a ratio of approximately 1:1.618 that recurs throughout traditional Squeakish design. The Council Platform, the Glassine Cliffs developments, and the floating quarters of Splashport are the most celebrated examples of contemporary Squeakish architecture.

The visual arts on Isoprene have been dominated, since the late pre-Federation period, by the Reedwater School and its various descendants. The Reedwater School emphasizes restrained palette, asymmetric composition, and the use of polyisoprene-based pigments that develop subtle color shifts over time. The Glassine School, by contrast, emphasizes the use of polychromatic crystal inlays and is closely associated with the architectural traditions of the Glassine Cliffs.

### 7.4 Sport

The most popular spectator sport on Isoprene is Stillwater Racing, in which teams of paddlers compete over standardized river courses. The Stillwater Grand Course, held annually on the Stillwater River, attracts an estimated audience of 600 million viewers across the Federation.

Other major sports include Marshball (a team sport played in the marshes of the Reedwater basin), Bubble-Fencing (a martial art derived from the training practices of the pre-war Fleet), and Squeak-Choir (a competitive vocal sport in which teams of singers compete to produce the most precise and sustained harmonies).

## Part VIII: Environment and Sustainability

### 8.1 Environmental Status

Isoprene's environment is, by historical standards, in good condition. The major environmental indicators — atmospheric composition, freshwater quality, marine ecosystem health, and forest cover — have been stable or improving for the last six cycles. The Reedwater Conservation Act and the Polymer Industry Compact are widely credited with arresting and reversing the environmental degradation of the post-war industrial boom.

The principal ongoing environmental concerns are the slow but measurable acidification of the Great Squeak Ocean (attributed to increased atmospheric carbon dioxide from off-world industrial activity in adjacent systems), the encroachment of urban development on the Reedwater marshes, and the localized impact of the Buoyant Yards on the Buoyant Estuary ecosystem.

### 8.2 Conservation Areas

Approximately 28% of Isoprene's land area is designated as some form of conservation area. The largest is the Western Sapwood Conservation Zone (1.4 million square kilometers), followed by the Reedwater Marshes Conservation Area, the Glassine Cliffs Heritage Zone, and the Squeak Plateau Wilderness.

The Reedwater Marshes Conservation Area is the most heavily regulated. All economic activity within the Area is prohibited except for traditional Reed-of-Council harvesting under license from the Reedwater Conservation Authority. The Authority issues approximately 4,000 harvesting licenses per cycle, distributed among the thirteen Paddles in proportion to their historical claims.

### 8.3 Climate

Isoprene's climate has been broadly stable for the last several thousand cycles, with mean global temperature varying by less than 0.4°C over that period. The most recent long-term climatological assessment (the Glassine Climate Survey of the 4,407th Cycle) projects continued stability over the next millennium, conditional on the maintenance of current emissions controls.

The principal climate-relevant economic sectors are the polymer industry (a net carbon sink, due to the carbon storage in long-lived polymer products), the shipbuilding industry (a moderate net emitter), and aquaculture (effectively carbon-neutral).

## Part IX: Strategic Considerations

### 9.1 Defense Posture

Isoprene's defense rests on three layers: the First Squadron in geostationary orbit; the planetary Bubble Shield Array, comprising 47 ground stations distributed across the surface; and the civilian preparedness infrastructure maintained by the Isoprene Constabulary.

The planetary Bubble Shield Array can sustain a full planet-encompassing shield for approximately 14 standard days under continuous attack, or indefinitely under intermittent attack. The Array has never been activated in anger.

### 9.2 Continuity of Government

In the event of a successful attack on the Federation Capital Zone, continuity of government is provided for under the Stillwater Protocol of the 4,148th Cycle. The Protocol designates the Glassine Cliffs as the primary continuity site, with secondary sites at Sapwood and at the Latheria Colonial Capital.

The Stillwater Protocol has been activated for exercise purposes thirty-one times. It has never been activated for actual emergency.

### 9.3 Strategic Resources

Isoprene's principal strategic resources are its polyisoprene production, its shipbuilding capacity, and its concentration of higher-education and research institutions. The loss of any of these would significantly impair the Federation's military and economic capability. Accordingly, the Polymer Industry Compact, the Buoyant Yards Charter, and the Federation Research Institutes are all explicitly designated as Strategic National Assets under the Compact Amendment of the 4,201st Cycle.
