# Governance and Economy of the Quack-Federa-tion

This document describes the political, legal, and economic structures of the Quack-Federa-tion as of the 4,412th Standard Cycle. It is intended as a primer for incoming diplomatic staff, exchange scholars, and first-cycle administrative cadets.

## The Tide Council

The Tide Council is the supreme legislative body of the Quack-Federa-tion. It convenes on the floating Council Platform above the city of Splashport on Isoprene.

### Composition

The Council has 169 seats, allocated as follows:

- **117 Paddle Seats:** Nine seats from each of the thirteen Paddles, elected by direct Paddle vote on a six-cycle rotation.
- **40 Colonial Seats:** Apportioned among the chartered colony worlds (Latheria, Whirlpoolia's Dark Outpost, Wave-Rider, Perfuma, and twelve smaller settlements) by population, recalculated every twelve cycles.
- **10 Sectoral Seats:** Two each from the five organized exploration sectors, elected by the captains and senior officers of vessels permanently assigned to that sector.
- **2 Echo-Folk Observer Seats:** Non-voting. Held in perpetuity by the resonant elders of Echo-Rock.

The presiding officer is the First Paddler, elected by the full Council to a six-cycle term. The current First Paddler is Honored Quill Marsh-of-Reeds, of the Reedsmith Paddle, now in her second term.

### Procedure

Council sessions follow the Ripple Protocol: a proposal must propagate through three readings, separated by no fewer than three standard days each, to allow Paddle delegations to consult their constituencies. Emergency provisions allow for an accelerated single-reading vote in cases of military necessity, but only with a four-fifths majority to invoke.

Voting is by raised bill (open) for procedural matters and by sealed reed-ballot (closed) for matters of war, treaty, or constitutional amendment.

## The Hydromarch

The Hydromarch is the executive officer of the Quack-Federa-tion, responsible for the day-to-day administration of the federation, command of the Fleet in peacetime, and conduct of foreign affairs. The Hydromarch is selected by the Tide Council from a slate of three candidates nominated by the standing Admiralty Board, serves a single eight-cycle term, and may not be reappointed.

The current Hydromarch is Vice-Commodore Plumage Stillwater, formerly captain of the survey vessel *Indefatigable Bubble*. Her term concludes in the 4,418th Cycle.

The Hydromarch presides over five Departments:

1. **The Department of the Inner Tide** — internal affairs, colonial administration, infrastructure.
2. **The Department of the Outer Tide** — foreign affairs, treaties, the Office of Xenology.
3. **The Admiralty** — the Fleet, the Marine Reserve, the Yards.
4. **The Department of Currents** — economy, trade, the Mint.
5. **The Department of Stillness** — justice, the courts, the Constabulary.

## The Courts

The Quack-Federa-tion operates a three-tier judicial system:

- **Pool Courts** — courts of first instance, present in every settlement of more than one thousand citizens. Presided over by a single elected magistrate.
- **Eddy Courts** — appellate courts, one per Paddle and one per colony world. Three-judge panels.
- **The Still Water** — the supreme court of the Federation. Nine justices, appointed for life by the Hydromarch with the consent of two-thirds of the Tide Council. The Still Water meets in chambers carved into the Glassine Cliffs of Isoprene.

Quack-Federa-tion law derives from three sources, in descending order of authority: the Founding Charter (the *Compact of the Reeds*, 3,021st Cycle), Council Statute, and Paddle Custom. Where these conflict, the higher source prevails.

## Citizenship

Citizenship is conferred at hatching to any individual born within Quack-Federa-tion space to at least one citizen parent, or by naturalization after a residency period of twenty cycles. Naturalized citizens may not stand for the Tide Council or the office of Hydromarch but otherwise enjoy full rights.

The Echo-Folk hold a unique status: collective citizenship, in which the species as a whole is recognized as a single citizen-entity for purposes of taxation and representation. This compromise was negotiated by the Treaty of the Long Hum in the 3,847th Cycle.

## Economy

The Quack-Federa-tion economy is a regulated market system overseen by the Department of Currents. The basic unit of currency is the Drop, subdivided into one hundred Splashes.

### Major Sectors

- **Shipbuilding.** Centered on Elastos and Isoprene. The principal yards are the Bouyant Yards (Splashport) and the Vulcanized Works (Elastos Prime). Federation shipbuilding consumed approximately 14% of total economic output in the most recent census cycle.
- **Aquaculture.** The dominant primary industry of Latheria and Wave-Rider. The Federation is a net exporter of farmed kelp, fingerlings, and pearl-grade tidemetal.
- **Polymer Extraction.** Isoprene's polyisoprene sap and Elastos's vulcanized polymers are the source of nearly all flexible structural materials used in Federation construction.
- **Tourism and Recreation.** Puddle is the most-visited recreational destination in the Federation, receiving approximately 4 million visitor-cycles per standard cycle.
- **Aromatics.** Perfuma's distilleries supply the Federation's entire luxury scent market and a growing export market to the few non-Federation polities the Quack-Federa-tion trades with.

### Trade Policy

The Quack-Federa-tion maintains a policy of cautious openness. Trade with the Absorbant Empire of Spong is prohibited under the post-Loofah armistice and remains so. Limited bilateral trade exists with the Wave-Rider city-states (technically Federation members but with substantial commercial autonomy) and with independent merchant consortia operating out of the Faucet Flow sector.

Tariffs are administered by the Bureau of Currents and are typically low (under 5%) for intra-Federation goods and substantially higher (15-40%) for goods originating outside Federation space.

## The Fleet

The Quack-Federa-tion Fleet is organized into seven Squadrons, each commanded by a Commodore reporting to the Admiralty. The current Fleet strength is approximately 340 capital vessels, 1,200 escorts, and an unknown number of small craft.

The Fleet's standing doctrine since the Battle of the Loofah Line emphasizes maneuver, defensive depth (via Bubble Shield arrays), and the use of the Wave Generator for area denial rather than direct engagement. The Fleet has not fought a major action in 391 standard cycles.
