# Compendium of Known Sapient Species

A short-form reference for the sapient and proto-sapient species cataloged by the Quack-Federa-tion Office of Xenology, current as of the 4,412th Standard Cycle.

## Anseriformes Sapiens (the Ducks)

The founding and dominant species of the Quack-Federa-tion. Originating on Isoprene, the Ducks are a semi-aquatic, bipedal species with feathered integument and a distinctive bill structure that doubles as a resonant chamber for their squeak-based language. Average lifespan is 142 standard cycles. Their physiology is hydrodynamically optimized, which historically informed the design of both their architecture and their starship hulls.

Culturally, the Ducks are organized into thirteen Paddles (clan-analogues), each tracing lineage to one of the original molding-pools of The Mold. Paddle membership is non-territorial and follows matrilineal descent.

## Spongiformes Vulgaris (the Spong)

A porous, colonial species native to the Sudsy Reaches beyond the Loofah Line. Each Spong individual is a loose aggregate of cellular units bound by a chitinous lattice; individuals can split, merge, or shed mass without loss of identity. This makes population counts essentially meaningless and is the source of much of the difficulty in negotiating with the Absorbant Empire of Spong.

The Spong communicate by chemical signaling through expressed mucilage. Their aggression toward the Quack-Federa-tion is historically rooted in a deep cultural revulsion toward impermeable surfaces, which they regard as a violation of the natural exchange between an organism and its medium.

## Cetacius Profundus (the Deep-Singers)

Colossal aquatic megafauna inhabiting the planet Deluge. The Deep-Singers are sapient but pre-technological, communicating through low-frequency songs that propagate around Deluge's globe-spanning ocean. A single song can take 47 cycles to complete one circumnavigation.

The Quack-Federa-tion maintains an observational embassy in Deluge's upper thermocline but does not formally recognize the Deep-Singers as a polity, as they have expressed no interest in interstellar affairs.

## Lithoforma Echonensis (the Echo-Folk)

A silicon-based species native to the cave systems of Echo-Rock. The Echo-Folk are slow-moving crystalline organisms that communicate by inducing resonant frequencies in the rock around them. A single conversation can last for several standard months.

They are a member species of the Quack-Federa-tion, holding two non-voting seats on the Tide Council. Their primary cultural export is a form of seismic poetry known as the Long Hum.

## Florafibra Bristlensis (the Bristlers)

The aggressive flora of the planet Bristle. Long classified as non-sapient, the Bristlers were reclassified in the 4,201st Cycle following the work of Doctoral-Drake Pendweather, who demonstrated coordinated tactical behavior across kilometer-scale plant networks. Whether this constitutes sapience or merely distributed reflex remains contested.

The Quack-Federa-tion currently maintains a no-contact protocol with Bristle, codified in the Pendweather Accord.

## Vapores Mistralis (the Mistral Wisps)

Atmospheric microorganisms native to Mistral that, when assembled in sufficient density, exhibit emergent cognition. A "Wisp" in this colloquial sense may comprise trillions of individual organisms acting in concert.

Wisps are short-lived — a single emergent intelligence typically persists for only a few standard days before dispersing — but they reliably re-form, often with apparent continuity of memory. The mechanism is not understood.

## Precursors (extinct, hypothesized)

A pre-Federation civilization of unknown morphology, known only through scattered artifacts. The Bar (orbiting Latheria) is the largest confirmed Precursor artifact. Precursor technology, where recovered, predates the Quack-Federa-tion by an estimated 18,000 cycles and operates on principles still not fully understood.

The Office of Xenology classifies the Precursors as Tier-0 Significance, meaning that any recovered artifact is to be reported to the Tide Council within one standard cycle.
