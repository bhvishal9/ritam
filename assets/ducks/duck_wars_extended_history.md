# The Great Duck Wars: An Extended History

This volume expands upon the abbreviated narrative in *The Great Duck Wars* (Quack-Federa-tion Historical Archive, Document QFH-0014) with the benefit of materials declassified following the 4,400th Cycle Archival Reform.

## Volume I: Origins

### Chapter 1: The Sudsy Reaches Before First Contact

For the first nine hundred cycles of Quack-Federa-tion expansion, the Sudsy Reaches beyond the Loofah Line were known only as a hazard. Survey vessels reported anomalous chemical readings — high concentrations of long-chain surfactants, complex polysaccharides, and what would later be identified as Spong reproductive spore. No one suspected sapient life. The prevailing theory, advanced by the natural philosopher Doctoral-Drake Pendweather (the great-grandsire of the Doctoral-Drake Pendweather who later worked on Bristle), held that the Reaches were a vast, slow-moving chemical reactor — a kind of cosmic fermentation vat.

The first hint of intelligence came from the survey vessel *Glossy Feather* in the 3,962nd Cycle. The *Glossy Feather*'s mass spectrometer recorded what appeared to be patterned modulation in the spore-density readings — patterns too regular for a natural phenomenon. The vessel's captain, Commander Marshweave, filed a curiosity report but recommended no follow-up. The report sat in the Office of Xenology archive for forty-one cycles.

### Chapter 2: The Reedsong Expedition

In the 4,003rd Cycle, the Tide Council authorized the Reedsong Expedition — a three-vessel survey under the joint command of Captain Burble Tidewalker and Captain Quill Marsh-of-Reeds (the great-grandam of the current First Paddler). The expedition's mandate was to "characterize the chemical and biological substrate of the Sudsy Reaches with a view to potential commercial exploitation."

The Reedsong Expedition entered the Sudsy Reaches on the 14th day of the 9th month of the 4,003rd Cycle. Within eleven standard days, the expedition reported the first confirmed sighting of a Spong aggregate large enough to be visually identified as an organism — a roughly spherical mass approximately three kilometers in diameter, drifting in a slow rotation through a particularly dense surfactant cloud.

The expedition attempted contact via chemical signaling, broadcasting standard Federation greeting esters into the surrounding medium. The aggregate's response, after a delay of approximately six standard days, was a coordinated reorientation toward the expedition's position and a sustained increase in the emission of what the expedition's chemist identified as a class of compounds with strong solvent properties.

Captain Tidewalker, interpreting this as a potentially hostile act, ordered the expedition to withdraw. The Reedsong Expedition returned to Federation space without losses but with a great deal of data.

### Chapter 3: The Period of Cautious Approach

The Tide Council debated the Reedsong findings for eleven cycles. Two factions emerged. The Open Channel faction, led by Councilor Tern Saltreed of the Saltreed Paddle, argued for sustained diplomatic engagement, on the theory that the Spong's apparent hostility was a misunderstanding rooted in fundamentally different communication modalities. The Closed Hull faction, led by Councilor Drake Hardshell of the Hardshell Paddle, argued for a defensive perimeter along the Loofah Line and a moratorium on further contact.

The compromise, codified in the Saltreed-Hardshell Resolution of the 4,015th Cycle, authorized continued contact attempts under heavy escort, with strict rules of engagement. Over the next thirty cycles, sixteen formal contact attempts were made. None produced a stable communication channel, though several yielded the chemical-linguistic primitives that would eventually allow the post-war translators to make limited sense of Spong signaling.

The crucial breakthrough came in the 4,047th Cycle, when the linguist-chemist Doctora-Hen Pondreader proposed that Spong signaling was not propositional but performative — that is, the Spong did not transmit statements *about* the world but rather chemical *actions* that altered it. Under this model, the Spong responses to Federation greetings were not communications at all but reflexive defensive secretions. Pondreader argued that what looked like hostility was, from the Spong's perspective, no more aggressive than a Duck closing its eyes against bright light.

Pondreader's framework was widely accepted in the academic community but had little political effect. The Closed Hull faction was by then ascendant in the Tide Council, and the Loofah Line had been fortified.

## Volume II: The War

### Chapter 4: The First Splish-Splash

Open war began on the 3rd day of the 2nd month of the 4,051st Cycle. Three Spong aggregates, each estimated at over fifty kilometers in diameter, crossed the Loofah Line and attacked the Federation outpost at Suds-Alpha-3 (a small mining station, since destroyed and not rebuilt). The attack used the device that would become known as the Drainer — a coordinated chemical-and-gravitational mechanism that generated micro-vortices around the Federation ships, threatening to crush their hulls through accelerated pressure differentials.

The outpost's defensive squadron, comprising the destroyers *Earnest Paddler* and *Quiet Wake* and the picket-corvette *Sallow Reed*, engaged. The *Quiet Wake* was lost with all hands. The *Earnest Paddler* and *Sallow Reed* succeeded in extracting most of the outpost's civilian population before withdrawing.

The Tide Council declared a state of war on the 17th day of the 2nd month. Admiral Suds, commanding the largest Spong aggregate ever observed in motion, advanced toward the Loofah Line within the cycle.

### Chapter 5: The Long Withdrawal

The first eighteen months of the war went badly for the Quack-Federa-tion. The Spong's Drainer technology was effective against the standard Federation hull configuration of the era — a thin, lightweight, high-flexibility design optimized for hydrodynamic efficiency rather than pressure resistance. Federation losses in the first cycle of the war exceeded sixty capital vessels.

The strategic response, developed under Hydromarch Brackwater, was the Long Withdrawal: a fighting retreat from the Loofah Line back to a defensive arc anchored on the colony world of Latheria, the resource world of Elastos, and the homeworld of Isoprene. The Long Withdrawal sacrificed substantial Federation territory but allowed the Yards to reconfigure for the production of the heavy-hulled Mark-VII Battlebill, the first Federation vessel designed from the keel for pressure resistance.

The Long Withdrawal also saw the operational debut of the early Bubble Shield. The Mark-I Bubble Shield was unreliable — it failed catastrophically in approximately one engagement in three — but when it worked, it negated the Drainer entirely.

### Chapter 6: The Stalemate

By the 4,055th Cycle, the war had settled into a grinding stalemate along the defensive arc. The Spong could not breach the arc; the Federation could not push back across the Loofah Line. The economic and demographic strain on the Federation was severe. The strain on the Spong is unknown but is believed, on the basis of post-war chemical archaeology, to have been at least comparable.

The stalemate was punctuated by a series of inconclusive engagements: the First Battle of the Bar (4,056), the Whirlpoolia Incursion (4,057), the Second Battle of the Bar (4,059), and the Long Bombardment of Elastos (4,060-4,062). None of these engagements significantly altered the strategic situation.

During the stalemate, a young officer named Waddles — at the time a Lieutenant-Commander aboard the *Indefatigable Bubble*, then a frontline cruiser — began circulating a series of tactical memoranda within the Admiralty arguing that the war could be ended by maneuver rather than attrition. The memoranda were initially dismissed as the work of a junior officer with insufficient operational experience. They were rediscovered and re-evaluated in the 4,063rd Cycle by the newly appointed Hydromarch Stillwater (the great-grandsire of the current Hydromarch), who promoted Waddles to Commodore and gave him operational command of the newly constituted Maneuver Squadron.

### Chapter 7: The Waddles Plan

Commodore Waddles's plan, in retrospect, was straightforward: induce the Spong to commit their main fleet to a location of Federation choosing, then use the recently perfected Mark-IV Wave Generator to drive the engaged Spong forces into the Loofah Line, where the abrasive cosmic scrub-material would do what Federation weapons could not.

The location Waddles chose was a region of relatively open space immediately spinward of the Loofah Line, in what is now called the Waddles Salient. The bait was a deliberate, public, and apparently incompetent feint by the Maneuver Squadron toward the Spong-held world of Suds-Beta-2. The Spong, under Admiral Suds, committed approximately three-quarters of their main fleet to intercept.

The trap was sprung on the 22nd day of the 7th month of the 4,063rd Cycle. The full account of the Battle of the Loofah Line is given in Chapter 8.

### Chapter 8: The Battle of the Loofah Line

The Maneuver Squadron, comprising forty-three capital vessels and approximately one hundred escorts, engaged the Spong main fleet at 04:14 standard time. The engagement opened with a sustained Drainer barrage from the Spong, which the Federation's improved Mark-V Bubble Shields largely negated.

At 04:47, Waddles ordered the Wave Generator activation. The Mark-IV Wave Generator, deployed across nine specially modified vessels operating in synchronized array, generated a coordinated gravitational tide of unprecedented magnitude. The resulting wave — the Waddles Wave — propagated outward from the Federation formation at approximately 0.14c, carrying with it the engaged Spong aggregates.

Spong aggregates, lacking rigid structure, could not maneuver against the wave. Within twenty-eight standard minutes, the entirety of the engaged Spong fleet had been driven into the inner edge of the Loofah Line. Spong losses are estimated at over 90% of engaged forces, including Admiral Suds, who was recovered alive by the Federation cruiser *Patient Paddler*.

Federation losses were eleven capital vessels and approximately forty escorts. The Maneuver Squadron's flagship, the *Indefatigable Bubble*, survived undamaged.

### Chapter 9: The Armistice

The capture of Admiral Suds proved decisive. Spong command-and-control, never well understood, appeared to depend in some essential way on the participation of the largest individual aggregates. With Suds in Federation custody and most of the rest of the senior Spong leadership dispersed across the Loofah Line, the Absorbant Empire was unable to mount coordinated military operations.

Armistice negotiations began on the 9th day of the 9th month of the 4,063rd Cycle, conducted through the chemical-linguistic translation framework Pondreader had developed decades earlier. The negotiations took eleven cycles to conclude.

The Loofah Accord was signed in the 4,074th Cycle. Its principal provisions:

1. The Loofah Line is designated a permanent neutral zone. Neither party may station military forces within twelve light-cycles of its boundary.
2. The Quack-Federa-tion renounces any claim to the Sudsy Reaches.
3. The Absorbant Empire of Spong renounces any claim to Federation space.
4. Admiral Suds is returned to Spong custody, in exchange for the return of the surviving crews of *Quiet Wake* and the other Federation vessels lost in the First Splish-Splash.
5. No formal diplomatic or commercial relations are established. Communication, where necessary, is conducted through the neutral offices of the Wave-Rider city-states.

The Loofah Accord remains in force.

## Volume III: Aftermath

### Chapter 10: The Reckoning Cycles

The decade following the armistice — the Reckoning Cycles — was a period of intense political and cultural introspection within the Quack-Federa-tion. The war had killed an estimated 340,000 citizens, displaced more than two million, and consumed approximately a third of accumulated Federation wealth. The strategic culture that had produced the disastrous Closed Hull posture of the pre-war period was thoroughly discredited.

The Reckoning Cycles produced the constitutional reforms of the 4,082nd Cycle, which expanded the Tide Council from 121 to 169 seats, established the modern five-Department executive structure, and codified the rules of engagement that govern Federation military doctrine to this day.

### Chapter 11: The Veterans

The veterans of the Maneuver Squadron formed a powerful political bloc in the post-war period. Commodore Waddles himself declined all political office, retiring to a houseboat on the Stillwater River on Isoprene, where he wrote his *Reflections on Tides*, a meditation on the moral burdens of command that remains required reading at the Naval Academy. Waddles died in the 4,098th Cycle. He was 156.

Other veterans were more politically active. Captain (later Commodore) Reed Bowsprit served three terms on the Tide Council and was instrumental in the passage of the Veterans' Pondsharing Act of the 4,089th Cycle, which established the modern Federation pension system. Lieutenant-Commander Marsh Pinfeather founded the Pinfeather Foundation, which to this day funds the chemical-linguistic research begun by Doctora-Hen Pondreader.

### Chapter 12: The Long Watch

The Quack-Federa-tion has been at peace for 391 standard cycles. The Loofah Line remains a neutral zone; the Sudsy Reaches remain closed to Federation traffic. Periodic reports of Spong aggregate movements, transmitted through Wave-Rider intermediaries, have so far never escalated into a violation of the Accord.

The Federation maintains the Long Watch: a permanent observational presence along the Federation side of the neutral zone, comprising approximately forty vessels at any given time. The Long Watch is rotated through the Fleet on a four-cycle assignment basis. Service on the Long Watch is regarded as honorable but uneventful, and is often used as a training assignment for newly commissioned officers.

Whether the peace will hold indefinitely is a question that the Office of the Outer Tide refuses to answer. The standing instruction to the Fleet, issued by Hydromarch Stillwater on the day of the armistice and never rescinded, is simply: *Watch the tide. Do not be the one to break it.*
