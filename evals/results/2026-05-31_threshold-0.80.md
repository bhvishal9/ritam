## Observations

- **Coverage collapses to 0.206.** Roughly 80% of retrieval queries now return nothing at all — most factual queries are below the 0.80 cosine ceiling. Recall drops from 0.889 → 0.222 on factual.
- **Multi-hop collapses to zero.** Requiring two specific docs to *both* clear 0.80 is essentially impossible for cross-doc queries, where the second doc's signal is always weaker than the dominant one.
- **Abstention is nearly perfect.** 14 of 15 out-of-scope queries abstain. The one false positive is worth inspecting — it's a query the retriever found a >0.80-similar chunk for, which means either the chunk is genuinely relevant (and the row should be reclassified) or the embedding model is producing a misleadingly high score (and that's a model-level diagnostic).

## Conclusion

0.80 buys abstention at the cost of nearly all retrieval. This is not an operating point — it's a data point on the precision/recall curve. The corpus's median in-domain similarity sits below 0.80, so this threshold filters out true positives at the same rate as true negatives.
